Common causes of clogged drains and how to prevent them

Common causes of clogged drains and how to prevent them

Posted by on 2024-08-19

Clogged drains are a common annoyance that many homeowners have to deal with. Whether it's in the kitchen sink, bathroom sink, or shower drain, a clog can disrupt your daily routine and cause frustration. There are several common causes of clogged drains, but the good news is that most of them are preventable with some simple maintenance.

One of the main culprits behind clogged drains is food particles and grease in the kitchen sink. When you wash dishes or rinse off plates before putting them in the dishwasher, bits of food can easily get stuck in the drain pipe and lead to a blockage over time. To prevent this, make sure to scrape off excess food into the trash before washing dishes and avoid pouring grease down the drain.

In the bathroom, hair is often the culprit behind clogged drains. Every time you shower or brush your hair over the sink, strands of hair can accumulate in the drain pipe and create a blockage. To prevent this, consider using a hair catcher in your shower or installing a mesh screen over your sink drain to catch any stray hairs.

Another common cause of clogged drains is soap scum and mineral buildup. Over time, soap residue can build up on the walls of your pipes and trap other debris passing through, leading to a blockage. To prevent this, try using enzyme-based drain cleaners regularly to break down any buildup before it becomes a problem.

To keep your drains flowing smoothly, it's important to practice good maintenance habits like avoiding flushing anything other than toilet paper down toilets and periodically cleaning out debris from sink stoppers and shower screens. By taking these simple steps to prevent clogs, you can save yourself from dealing with costly plumbing repairs down the line. Remember, prevention is key when it comes to keeping your drains clear and free-flowing!
